Jinha
Yoon

I'm a PhD candidate in the ART-AI Centre for Doctoral Training (Accountable, Responsible & Transparent AI) at the University of Bath, UK. My research focuses on the intersection of human-computer interaction, AI ethics, and user experience design.

While much of the AI field focuses on improving technical performance, my work addresses a different gap: the disconnect between how AI systems are designed and how real users understand, trust, and act on the AI recommendations. To investigate this, I designed an LLM-powered ‘AI doctor’ in immersive VR, a patient-facing interface that augments clinical workflows while preserving the social and contextual cues that matter to lay users. Through this system, I study how the information an AI communicates shapes patient trust in clinical decision making, with direct implications for AI governance and responsible deployment.

I grew up in Japan and China, and now live in the UK. When I'm not doing research, I run, play tennis, watch F1 and travel.
